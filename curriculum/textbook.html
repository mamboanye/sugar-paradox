The data

37 Sub-Saharan African countries, 2010-2022. Sugar supply, vegetable oil, obesity, diabetes, GDP, urbanization. 962 rows. All public.

The paradox

Countries with more sugar are fatter (r = 0.68). Follow them over time and the signal vanishes. Six within-country specifications, all null.

The method

Each chapter teaches one regression concept. Each concept produces one number from the paper. You learn statistics by dismantling the paradox.

The journey

Start at t = 19.84 (huge). End at t = -0.13 (dead). Fifteen chapters. One dataset. Every widget uses real data.

Findings during construction

  • Paper framing: The paper says "the only predictor" of obesity change is initial obesity (t = 3.45). Urbanization (t = 1.73, p = 0.09) and oil change (t = -1.84, p = 0.08) are marginal at 10%. Defensible at 5%, but a framing choice. (Ch. 15)
  • Bootstrap nuance: Script 12 uses unrestricted residuals for the wild cluster bootstrap. The strict version uses restricted residuals. Negligible difference here (beta = -0.029) but pedagogically important. (Ch. 10)
  • Zero data errors across all 15 chapters. Every number verified against scripts and the paper.
